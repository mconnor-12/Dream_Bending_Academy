📓 Note Catcher Reference

The thinking tools we use every day

Print Class Set:
C
CharactersFill in from the text: name, age, traits. Add arrows → to show how characters change across settings. Track up to 3 characters.
S
SettingTrack 2–3 settings. Jot quick details as you go — farm home, army camp, Washington. Note how setting shifts affect character.
P
ProblemList up to 3 problems in sequence. Note who has the problem and what they want. Track problem → solution arrows.
S
SolutionFor each problem: does a solution appear? Partial or full? Use arrows and quick jots — don't copy full evidence yet.
LL
Lesson LearnedWrite 1 sentence: the universal truth the story teaches. Connect to the character's journey. This becomes your theme claim.

🟥 Fill in from the text  ·  🟩 Jots/details you learn as you go  ·  Write names on the whole-class side

G=NF
GenreLabel the genre: Non-Fiction. Note your first/last name and homeroom in the top-right corner.
BT
Big TopicLook at TITLE and TOPIC TANGLES. Write the big topic — what is the whole text about? Use the title and headings to guide you.
T
Teach — TopicsTrack up to 4 section topics. For each: jot key details and evidence IDs. DON'T copy full evidence — just quick IDs to locate it.
APOV
Author's POVArguments 1 & 2: What is the author trying to prove? Audience: Who are they writing to and why? Note why → audience arrows.
MI
Main IdeaWrite the main idea in 1–2 sentences. This is the author's central message — connect BT + APOV to form it.

🟥 Organizes your sections  ·  🟩 Quick jots + evidence IDs (don't copy all evidence)

R
Restate the QuestionRestate the question in your own words. This becomes the first sentence of your response. Don't copy — transform it into your own statement.
A
Answer the QuestionGive your direct answer / claim. Be specific — don't be vague. This is your position that the rest of the response proves.
C¹
Cite Evidence #1Choose your strongest quote. Introduce it ("According to the text…" or "In paragraph __, it states…"). Use exact words that prove your answer.
C²
Cite Evidence #2Find a second piece of evidence — different from the first. It can be from the same or a different paragraph. Introduce it with a new phrase.
E
Explain How Evidence Supports Your AnswerConnect BOTH pieces of evidence back to your answer. Why does this evidence matter? What does it show, reveal, or prove about your claim?

Based on the RACCE framework  ·  R · A · C¹ · C² · E  ·  Always check: does your E connect BOTH pieces of evidence back to your A?

POE CODES — Use on every MC question

LE — Lacks Evidence
DC — Doesn't Connect
TS — Too Specific
